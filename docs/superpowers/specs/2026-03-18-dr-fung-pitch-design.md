# Dr. Fung Pitch — Slides & Script Design
**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Goal:** A polished but warm 8-slide PowerPoint deck with a word-for-word script to pitch Dr. Hank Fung on the redesigned Pacific Dermatology website.

---

## Context

A luxury redesign of Dr. Fung's site is live at `https://pacific-dermatology-g5jw.vercel.app/`. The developer is a former patient pitching this on a Zoom call. The goal is to land $750 as a one-time build fee, with an optional retainer for ongoing SEO/growth work presented as a soft mention — not a hard ask.

**Tone:** Polished but warm. Word-for-word script for key moments (opening, reveal, story, ask, close). Loose talking points for the walkthrough.

**Pricing:**
- Build fee: $750 one-time (framed as "portfolio pricing" vs. $8–15k agency rate)
- Flexibility signaled: "I'm open to talking about it" — not desperation, just warmth
- Retainer: optional, month-to-month, presented only if he's engaged, not a hard ask

---

## Slide Structure

### Slide 1 — Title: "I built something for you."
**Content:** Developer name, date, live URL.
**Purpose:** Set a personal, gift-like tone before anything else.

**Script (word for word):**
> "Hey Dr. Fung, thanks for making time. I'll keep this to 20 minutes. I was actually a patient of yours a few years back — [brief personal detail]. I'm now doing web development, and I built something for your practice as a project. I'd love to just show it to you and get your honest reaction."

**Note:** Don't explain what you built yet. Just show. Fill in the personal detail before the call — a specific memory ("came in for a mole check," "you treated my eczema") lands far better than a vague mention.

---

### Slide 2 — His Current Site (Screenshot)
**Content:** Full-page screenshot of pacific-dermatology.com. No labels, no commentary in the slide.
**Purpose:** Let the visual gap land before the reveal.

**Script (word for word):**
> [Pull up the screenshot. Say nothing. Let 3–4 seconds pass.]
> "This is what patients see when they Google you today."

**Note:** Do NOT say anything negative. If he defends it, nod — "totally, it gets the job done" — and move on immediately.

---

### Slide 3 — The New Site (Live Screen Share)
**Content:** Near-blank slide — just the URL in small text at the bottom as a reference. The audience sees almost nothing; this is a transition cue for the presenter. Switch tabs immediately. No bullet points, no heading.
**Purpose:** The visual reveal. Highest-impact moment of the pitch.

**Script (word for word):**
> "And this is what I built."
> [Switch browser tabs. Pause on the hero for 4–5 seconds. Let him look.]
> "I'll walk you through it in a second — but what's your first impression?"

**Note:** Ask for his reaction BEFORE the walkthrough. His words reveal engagement level and set the tone for the rest.

---

### Slide 4 — Live Walkthrough: Homepage → Services → Contact
**Content:** Stay on the live site. This is a guided tour, not a slide.
**Purpose:** Let him feel ownership of the site.

**Talking points (loose):**
- **About section:** "I used your actual headshot — wanted it to feel like your practice, not a template."
- **Services page:** "Every treatment you offer is listed — medical and cosmetic. I cross-referenced your current site."
- **Contact page:** "Address, phone, a contact form, and Google Maps. Patients can reach you without hunting."
- **Check in:** "Does this feel like it represents your practice?"

**Note:** Keep it under 4 minutes. Only go deeper on what he asks about.

---

### Slide 5 — Your Story: Former Patient → Web Developer
**Content:** Minimal slide — developer name, "Former Patient → Web Developer."
**Purpose:** Establish trust and reframe the pitch as a personal gesture, not a sales call.

**Script (word for word):**
> "So here's the honest story. I was your patient — I genuinely trust you as a doctor. When I started learning web development, I wanted a real project to build, not just a tutorial. Your practice came to mind because I know the care you put into it, and I thought it deserved a better online presence. So I just built it. No strings attached."
> [Pause.]
> "I do have a proposal if you'd like to hear it — but even if not, I'm happy to hand this over."

**Note:** The last line is critical — it lowers stakes and signals zero pressure. Only move to pricing if he gives a green light (a nod counts).

---

### Slide 6 — The Ask
**Content:**
- Price: **$750** — one-time, site is theirs, hosted, live under their domain
- Market anchor: "Agencies: $8,000–$15,000 + $1,000/mo"

**Script (word for word):**
> "I want to be upfront. I'm newer to freelancing and you're genuinely someone I wanted to build this for — so I'm not coming in with agency pricing. Agencies that do this for dermatology practices charge $8–15k to build and $1,000+ a month after that.
>
> I'm proposing $750, one time. That covers the build, I handle the Vercel hosting, and I get your domain pointed to this site within a week — you just give me DNS access, which takes about five minutes on your end."
>
> "And honestly — if the number doesn't work for you, I'm open to talking about it."

**Note:** "I'm open to talking about it" signals warmth and flexibility — not desperation. It's a deliberate choice.

---

### Slide 7 — Optional: If You Want to Keep Growing It (Retainer)
**Content:**
- Individual SEO pages per treatment ("Kybella Pleasanton," "Daxxify Pleasanton," etc.)
- Google Analytics monthly traffic report
- Google Business Profile updates
- Before/after gallery, online booking (when ready)
- Month-to-month, no lock-in
- Soft monthly range if he asks: **$150–$250/month** depending on scope

**Purpose:** Plant the seed for ongoing work. Only show if he's visibly engaged — skip or skim if energy is neutral.

**Script (loose):**
> "If you ever want to take this further — this is what that looks like. The big SEO opportunity is individual pages for each treatment. When someone Googles 'Kybella Pleasanton' or 'Daxxify Pleasanton,' you want to show up. That's about 15–20 pages, Month 2 type work. We'd do it month-to-month, no long commitment. But that's all optional — the $750 stands on its own."

---

### Slide 8 — Close: What Happens Next
**Content:** Two bullet points — "Live in a week." / "Five minutes on your end."

**Script (word for word):**
> "If you want to move forward, I can have this live under pacific-dermatology.com within a week. I just need DNS access on your end — it's about five minutes, I walk you through it.
>
> If you want some time to think or show your staff, totally fine — I'll send you the link right after this call so you can pull it up whenever.
>
> Either way, I appreciate you taking the time. Genuinely."

**Note:** Do NOT ask "so what do you think?" Give a concrete next step (DNS) or a graceful out (share link with staff). Both lead somewhere. If he asks about hosting costs: Vercel's free Hobby plan covers this site's traffic — no ongoing cost to pass on.

## Objection Responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I already have a website" | "I know — that's actually why I built this. I wanted to show you what it could look like." |
| "I don't get many patients from my website" | "That's partly because it's hard to find in Google. This is built to change that — especially once we add individual pages for each treatment." |
| "Can I think about it?" | "Of course. I'll send you the link right after this so you can show your staff." Follow up by phone in 3 business days. |
| "That seems expensive" | "To put it in context — a single Sculptra or Kybella patient covers the whole build fee. And agencies charge ten times this." |
| "I'm happy with what I have" | "Totally fair. I'm happy to leave the link with you — no obligation at all." |

## PowerPoint Slide Design Guidelines

**Aesthetic:** Match the site — dark navy background (`#1c2331`), gold accents (`#c9963e`), cream text (`#fdfbf7`). Playfair Display for headings, clean sans-serif for body.

**Slide rules:**
- Max 1 idea per slide
- No bullet walls — talking points stay in the speaker notes, not on screen
- Slides 3 and 4 are screen-share moments — the slide deck takes a back seat
- Slide 6 (The Ask): price is the ONLY number on screen — large, centered, impossible to miss

**Speaker notes:** Every slide's script goes in the PowerPoint speaker notes so it's visible during the Zoom without the audience seeing it.
